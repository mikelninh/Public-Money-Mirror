## 1. Purpose

This Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) establishes the terms for a pilot engagement between Public Money Mirror and [Client] to identify and recover savings in public procurement spending through data-driven anomaly detection and recovery services.

## 2. Pilot Scope

### 2.1 Data Access
- Client grants access to public procurement data for the period: [Start Date] to [End Date]
- Data includes: tenders, awards, contracts, and budget allocations
- Data will be provided in [format: CSV, API, etc.]

### 2.2 Analysis Period
- Pilot duration: [X] months
- Initial analysis delivery: Within [Y] weeks of data receipt
- Ongoing monitoring: Monthly reports

### 2.3 Deliverables
- Ranked list of potential savings cases with evidence
- Recovery kits (benchmarks, alternatives, draft letters) for top cases
- Monthly summary reports

## 3. Success Fee Terms

### 3.1 Fee Structure
- **Rate**: [15% - 25%] of realised savings (to be specified)
- **Definition of "Realised Savings"**: Actual reductions in procurement costs achieved through:
  - Contract renegotiations
  - Alternative supplier agreements
  - Cancelled overpriced contracts
  - Verified budget adjustments

### 3.2 Measurement Period
- Savings realisation period: [X] months after case identification
- Verification: Independent audit or Client's finance team confirmation

### 3.3 Payment Terms
- Invoice issued within [X] days of savings verification
- Payment due: [30] days from invoice date
- Payment method: [Bank transfer, etc.]

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Provider (Public Money Mirror)
- Conduct analysis and identify potential savings
- Generate recovery kits and evidence dossiers
- Provide monthly progress reports
- Maintain data confidentiality and security

### 4.2 Client
- Provide access to procurement data
- Review cases and recovery kits
- Act on recommendations where feasible
- Report realised savings for invoicing

## 5. Data Protection & Confidentiality

- All data handled in accordance with GDPR
- Confidentiality: Neither party shall disclose sensitive procurement details publicly
- Data retention: [X] years or as required by law

## 6. Intellectual Property

- Analysis methodology: Property of Public Money Mirror
- Client data: Remains property of Client
- Anonymized insights: May be used by Provider for research/improvement

## 7. Pilot Timeline

- **Phase 1**: Data ingestion & analysis ([X] weeks)
- **Phase 2**: Case identification & ranking ([X] weeks)
- **Phase 3**: Recovery kit generation ([X] weeks)
- **Phase 4**: Implementation support ([X] months)

## 8. Success Metrics

Pilot considered successful if:
- [X] high-confidence cases identified (score > 75)
- At least €[X] in potential savings identified
- [X] recovery kits delivered
- [X] cases acted upon by Client

## 9. Termination

Either party may terminate with [30] days written notice.

## 10. Next Steps

Upon pilot completion:
- Review results and lessons learned
- Evaluate extension to full engagement
- Adjust success fee terms if needed
