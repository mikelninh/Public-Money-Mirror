# Success Fee Terms & Definitions

## Overview

Public Money Mirror operates on a success-fee model for government clients: we charge a percentage of **realised savings** achieved through our recommendations.

## Fee Structure

### Standard Rates

- **Pilot Phase**: 15-20% of realised savings
- **Full Engagement**: 18-25% of realised savings
- **Enterprise License**: Fixed annual fee (separate from success fees)

### Rate Determination

Rate depends on:
- Engagement scope and complexity
- Expected savings volume
- Client relationship and history
- Risk profile of identified cases

## Definitions

### Realised Savings

**Realised Savings** = Verified reduction in procurement costs achieved through actions taken based on Public Money Mirror's recommendations.

#### Includes:
- Contract renegotiations with existing suppliers (price reductions)
- Switching to alternative suppliers (cost differences)
- Cancelled overpriced contracts (savings from non-execution)
- Budget adjustments based on evidence
- Penalties recovered from suppliers (if applicable)

#### Excludes:
- **Potential savings** (not yet realised)
- **Projected savings** (estimates)
- Savings from actions not based on our recommendations
- Internal process improvements unrelated to our analysis

### Verification Period

- **Standard**: 6-12 months after case identification
- **Extended**: Up to 18 months for long-term contracts
- Verification method: Client's finance team or independent audit

### Measurement Methods

1. **Before/After Comparison**
   - Original contract value vs. renegotiated value
   - Original supplier quote vs. alternative supplier quote

2. **Budget Comparison**
   - Allocated budget vs. actual spend after recommendations

3. **Contract Value Changes**
   - Documented contract amendments with value changes

## Invoicing Process

### Timeline

1. **Case Identification**: Public Money Mirror identifies potential savings
2. **Recovery Kit Delivery**: Evidence and recommendations provided
3. **Client Action**: Client acts on recommendations (renegotiates, switches suppliers, etc.)
4. **Savings Realisation**: Actual cost reductions achieved
5. **Verification**: Client confirms savings amount
6. **Invoicing**: Invoice issued within 30 days of verification
7. **Payment**: Due within 30 days of invoice date

### Invoice Contents

- Case reference numbers
- Description of savings realised
- Verification documentation
- Calculation: `Savings Amount × Fee Rate = Invoice Amount`
- Payment terms and bank details

## Example Calculation

### Scenario

- **Case**: Overpriced office supplies contract
- **Original Contract**: €100,000/year
- **Renegotiated Contract**: €75,000/year (after our recommendation)
- **Realised Savings**: €25,000
- **Success Fee Rate**: 20%

### Invoice

- **Savings Realised**: €25,000
- **Fee Rate**: 20%
- **Invoice Amount**: €25,000 × 0.20 = **€5,000**

## Dispute Resolution

If Client disputes savings calculation:

1. Both parties review evidence
2. If unresolved, independent auditor reviews
3. Auditor's decision is binding

## Transparency

- All calculations documented
- Evidence available for audit
- Regular reporting on case status

## Payment Terms

- **Standard**: Net 30 days
- **Payment Method**: Bank transfer (details on invoice)
- **Currency**: EUR (or as specified in contract)

## Confidentiality

Savings details and case information remain confidential between parties unless required by public disclosure laws.
